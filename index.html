Income Guardrails
Dynamic rules that automatically adapt income to market conditions. Teal = protective, green = positive.
Guardrail settings
⏸
Pause refill  20%
Stop topping up Bucket 1 from Bucket 3 when growth assets are more than this % below their recent peak. Prevents forced selling at the bottom. Research default: 20%
↓
Flex-down  85%
Trigger a temporary 10% income reduction if the total portfolio falls to this % of its starting value. A protective adjustment — not a plan failure. Research default: 85%
↑
Flex-up  130%
Permanently raise income 10% when portfolio reaches this % of starting value. A one-way ratchet — the new level becomes the baseline. Research default: 130%

Most tax-efficient withdrawal strategy — adviser reference
25% tax-free cash (PCLS)
The 25% pension commencement lump sum (up to £268,275 lifetime limit) is tax-free. Key considerations:
Withdrawal sequencing strategy
1ISA first — withdrawals are tax-free with no impact on personal allowance or benefit entitlements. Ideal for topping up Bucket 1.
2PCLS / tax-free portion of UFPLS — draw the tax-free element of pension benefits to supplement ISA income without triggering income tax.
3Pension (FAD / taxable UFPLS) — taxable withdrawals should be sized to stay within the client's personal allowance plus basic rate band where possible.
4GIA last — only crystallise GIA gains after exhausting tax-free sources. Use the annual CGT allowance (£3,000) each year to manage gains. ebi FoF avoids CGT on internal rebalancing.

How this plan addresses the three key risks in retirement
📉
Sequence-of-returns risk
The danger of a market crash early in retirement
Protected

The single biggest threat in retirement is a major market fall in the first few years, before your portfolio has had time to recover. If you are forced to sell investments at depressed prices to fund income, you permanently reduce the capital base — the damage is irreversible.

How this plan protects you
Bucket 1 holds 2 years of income in cash — you never need to sell investments to pay the bills, even in a severe crash
Bucket 2 adds a further 5 years of reserves — combined runway of 7 years before Bucket 3 needs to be touched
The pause guardrail automatically stops refilling Bucket 1 from Bucket 3 when markets are down, preventing forced selling at the worst time
⏳
Longevity risk
The risk of outliving your money
Managed

Retirement can last 30 or 40 years. A portfolio that runs out of money in year 25 is no better than one that runs out in year 10. The plan needs to sustain real purchasing power for a potentially very long period.

How this plan protects you
Bucket 3 is invested for long-term real growth — over a 30-year horizon it is designed to sustain the strategy to age 95

📈
Inflation risk
The gradual erosion of purchasing power
Addressed

At 3% inflation, the cost of living doubles roughly every 24 years. A flat income in retirement is effectively a pay cut every single year. The plan must grow income over time to maintain the client's standard of living.

How this plan protects you
Bucket 3 targets real (above-inflation) returns — the growth engine is designed to outpace inflation over a full market cycle
Income is reviewed annually and increased in line with 3.0% assumed inflation, maintaining real purchasing power
The flex-up guardrail permanently raises income when the portfolio grows strongly — clients benefit directly from real returns above inflation
